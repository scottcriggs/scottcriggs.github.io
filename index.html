Book Notes

"Vivid Vison" by Cameron Herold

The primary purpose of the book is to provide a practical guide on how to inspire and align the entire organization. By sharing a clear and compelling vision, employees, stakeholders, and even customers can understand where the company is headed and their role in achieving that vision.

"An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management" by Will Larson.

Systems Thinking for Engineering Management: The book emphasizes the importance of understanding and designing effective systems for managing engineering teams. It goes beyond individual leadership skills and focuses on creating the right environment and processes for engineers to thrive. A must read for all engineering managers.

"Building a Story Brand" by Donald Miller

The book presents a framework called the "StoryBrand 7-Part Framework" (SB7) based on the structure of classic storytelling. Miller argues that customers are drawn to clear, compelling narratives, and businesses can use this framework to clarify their message and connect with their audience. The central principle of the SB7 Framework is that the customer should be the hero of the story, not the brand. The brand plays the role of the guide, helping the hero overcome challenges and achieve their goals. Clarity Over Cleverness: Miller emphasizes the importance of clarity in messaging. Businesses should avoid jargon, complex language, and confusing marketing messages. Instead, they should focus on communicating their value proposition in a simple, easy-to-understand way.

"Escaping the Build Trap: How Effective Product Management Creates Real Value" by Melissa Perri

The Build Trap is the central concept of the book. It refers to the common situation where companies become overly focused on shipping features (outputs) rather than on achieving desired outcomes and creating value for customers. Organizations caught in the build trap are essentially "feature factories," churning out products without a clear understanding of whether they are actually solving real problems or achieving business goals.
